# Luma — Cold Pitch Playbook

## The Walk-In Script

**Before you walk in:** Run their menu through Luma's audit tool. Print the one-page report. Know 2–3 specific dishes where they're likely leaving money on the table.

**The approach:** Go during a slow period (2–4 PM on a weekday). Ask to speak with the owner or manager. If they're not in, ask when they usually are and come back.

**The opener (30 seconds):**

> "Hey, my name's Ethan — I'm a data science student at Berkeley. I'm building a tool that helps local restaurants figure out their menu pricing, and I ran a free analysis on your menu as a test case. I found a couple of dishes where you might be leaving some money on the table. Mind if I show you? It'll take two minutes and it's totally free — I'm just looking for feedback on the tool."

**Key principles:**
- Lead with value, not with "buy my thing"
- You're a student asking for feedback, not a salesperson — that's disarming
- Have the printed one-pager ready to hand them
- Point to specific dishes: "Your [dish] is priced at $11.50, but the average on Telegraph for a similar [category] is $13.50. If you're running typical ingredient costs, that dish might be at 35–40% food cost. Bumping it $1.50 could save you $600–800 a month."
- If they push back on the estimates: "That's exactly why I'd love to plug in your real numbers — the estimates get us in the ballpark, but your actual supplier costs would make this precise."

**If they're interested:**

> "I can set this up with your actual ingredient costs — takes about an hour to go through your recipes. Once it's in, you can see exactly what every dish costs you and where your margins are. I charge $200 for the setup and $79 a month after that, but honestly I'm still building this out, so for my first few restaurants I'll do the setup free."

**If they're not interested:**

> "Totally understand. Mind if I leave this with you? If you ever want to take a look, my email's on the bottom."

Leave the one-pager. Move on. No pressure.

## Pricing Strategy

**Phase 1 (first 3 restaurants): Free.**
Do the setup for free in exchange for permission to use them as case studies on your site. You need the social proof more than the money right now.

**Phase 2 (restaurants 4–10): Low-cost.**
- Free audit (always free — it's the door-opener)
- $199 one-time setup (you input their real costs, build their recipes)
- $79/month for ongoing access, quarterly margin reviews, and price update alerts

**Phase 3 (scaling): Raise prices.**
Once you have case studies showing real results ("Restaurant X saved $1,200/month by repricing 4 dishes"), the tool sells itself at higher price points.

## What to Track

For each restaurant you work with, document:
- Before/after food-cost % on repriced dishes
- Monthly savings estimate
- Any qualitative feedback ("I had no idea my pad thai was losing money")
- Permission to use their name + results on your site

These become your case studies — the most valuable asset for selling to the next restaurant.
