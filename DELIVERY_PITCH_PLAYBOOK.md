# Luma — Delivery Profit Pitch Playbook

## The Counter Pitch (30 seconds, no appointment needed)

Walk into any restaurant that's on DoorDash. You don't need a meeting — you need their attention for 60 seconds.

**Before you walk in:** Pull up their DoorDash listing on your phone. Note 2–3 dishes that are likely losing money on delivery (high-ingredient-cost items priced under $16 on a 30% commission). Run them through Luma if possible.

**The opener:**

> "Hey, quick question — are you guys on DoorDash? [yes] Do you know which of your dishes actually make money on there after DoorDash takes their 30%? I built a tool that breaks it down per item. I ran yours — your [specific dish] is probably losing you about $1.50 every time someone orders it on DoorDash. Want to see the breakdown? Takes 60 seconds."

**Why this works:**
- You're not selling. You're showing them something about their own business they didn't know.
- The $1.50 loss is specific and visceral. It's not "your margins might be low" — it's "you lose a dollar fifty on this dish, every single order."
- Most restaurant owners have a vague sense that DoorDash takes too much, but they've never done the math at the item level. You just did it for them.
- The 30% commission is the villain, not you. You're on their side.

**If they look at the numbers:**

> "The fix is usually pretty simple. DoorDash lets you set different prices for delivery vs. dine-in — most Berkeley restaurants charge 15–20% more on delivery to offset the commission. You could also pull your worst items off the delivery menu entirely. I can set up the full system with your actual costs if you want exact numbers — but even with estimates, the pattern is pretty clear."

**If they're interested in more:**

> "I'm building this for local restaurants — right now I'm working with a few places in Berkeley. For my first few, I'm doing the full setup free in exchange for feedback. Takes about 30 minutes to go through your delivery menu together. Want to set up a time?"

**If they're not interested:**

> "No worries. Here's the one-pager — it shows the breakdown for your top items. My email's on the bottom if you ever want to look at it."

Leave it. Move on.

## The Phone Version (for DoorDash-native / ghost kitchen operators)

Some Berkeley restaurants are delivery-first or delivery-heavy. For these, the pitch is even sharper:

> "You're doing most of your volume through DoorDash, right? Do you know your per-item profit after commission? Because the math gets brutal on some dish types — anything with expensive protein under $16 is usually underwater at 30%. I built a tool that shows the exact number per dish. Can I send you a one-page breakdown?"

## Common Objections

**"We already know DoorDash takes too much."**
> "Totally — everyone knows the 30% hurts. But do you know *which items* are still profitable and which ones actually lose money? That's the part nobody's done the math on. Your [high-margin dish] makes great money on delivery. Your [low-margin dish] doesn't. The question is which ones to keep and which to cut or reprice."

**"We can't raise our delivery prices — customers will stop ordering."**
> "Most Berkeley restaurants on DoorDash are already charging 15–20% more for delivery vs. dine-in. Customers expect it — they know delivery costs more. You're actually underpriced for delivery right now compared to your neighbors."

**"We tried doing our own delivery to avoid the commission."**
> "Makes sense for some items. This tool can actually help with that too — it shows you which items are worth delivering yourself (high margin, worth the driver cost) and which are only worth doing through the platform (low-margin items where volume matters more)."

**"I don't have time for this."**
> "Totally get it. I'll leave this one-pager — it shows the per-item breakdown. Take a look when you get a chance, and if anything jumps out, my email's on the bottom."

## Pricing Strategy

**Phase 1 (first 3): Free.**
Full setup free. In exchange: permission to use their results as a case study, and a testimonial if they find it useful.

**Phase 2 (restaurants 4–10): Introductory.**
- Free delivery audit (always free)
- $149 one-time setup (input their real costs, optimize their delivery menu)
- $59/month for ongoing monitoring and quarterly optimization

**Phase 3 (scaling):**
- Free audit
- $299 setup
- $99/month
- Pitch with case studies: "Restaurant X cut 4 items from their DoorDash menu and saved $800/month."

## Metrics to Track

For each restaurant:
- Number of items flagged as money-losers
- Actions taken (items removed, prices raised, menu simplified)
- Before/after monthly delivery profit estimate
- Owner testimonial / quote
- Permission to use as case study (yes/no)

These become your pitch deck for the next restaurant.
